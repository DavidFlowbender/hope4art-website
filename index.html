About Culture Art Academy

Based in FESTAC Town, Lagos, Culture Art Academy nurtures talent and preserves cultural identity through art education, exhibitions, and community projects. Founded by Augustine Ekhaiyeme, we bridge tradition and contemporary expression while empowering the next generation of creatives.

We partner with schools, local governments, and cultural bodies to bring art into communities—sparking dialogue on identity, environment, and youth empowerment.

Hope 4 Art Foundation

Established in 2017 to reach disadvantaged youths with art education, mentorship and materials.

  • Art supplies & mentorship
  • Community outreach & talks
  • Platforms for exhibitions

Programs

A glimpse into our key creative programmes—kept concise for a clean page.

Art Residency Programme

Creative immersion for emerging & established artists with studio access, mentorship, and collaboration—culminating in an open studio or mini showcase.

  • Studio access & guided feedback
  • Community engagement
  • Open studio presentations

Summer Art Camp

Fun, supportive holiday studio for children & teens—drawing, painting, crafts, murals, illustration, and creative storytelling with professional guidance.

  • Hands-on learning & new friendships
  • Guided by professional artists
  • Holds annually: Aug 1–31

Art Teachers Exhibition — Freedom of Expression

We invite art teachers, instructors, and practicing artists to submit works for the second edition of the Art Teachers Exhibition, themed “Freedom of Expression.” This exhibition celebrates the dedication, creativity, and artistic identity of those who inspire others in the visual arts.

  • Date: November 29th, 2025
  • Venue: CRIMMD Museum of Nigerian History, Ajao Estate, Lagos
  • Eligibility: Art teachers, instructors, or mentors
  • Submission: Up to three works in any medium

Whether you’re currently teaching, have taught in the past, or mentor young artists — this is your platform to showcase your voice and vision.
